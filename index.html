Frequently Asked Questions

What is HackYSU?

HackYSU is a hackathon, an event where students come together to Learn, Create, and Network with each other and businesses.

What do people do at hackathons?

Students will have 24 or 36 hours to create anything they want – software, hardware, a business plan, art, a design, anything – which will be exhibited in a showcase at the end.

Does it cost anything to participate?

HackYSU will not only be free to attend, but we'll supply food, energy drinks, and even some travel reimbursement for those who qualify.

Are there any rules?

Participants must be currently enrolled in a university to participate. Also, in order to qualify for prizes, all showcased work must be original to the hackathon – code, build, and create here. It's okay to plan a bit beforehand, though!

Is this a competition?

Though HackYSU will have prizes for top hacks in certain categories, as long as you learned, created, or networked, you've achieved what the HackYSU team has set out to do.

Do I need to be able to code?

No! Not only is HackYSU not a programming only event, it is an educational experience. Pros and newbies alike are invited to expand their skill set and make something awesome.

How do teams work?

Official teams will consist of four or less people. Teams can be spontaneously created at the event or set beforehand during registration.

What should I bring?

Bring your student ID, laptop, chargers, and everything you need to be a decent human being for 36 hours. That includes toiletries and sleeping materials. Showers may or may not be available.
